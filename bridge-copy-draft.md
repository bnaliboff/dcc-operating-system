## 3. Glossary — 19 entries

**360 Review** — Quarterly calibration where Functional Leader, Pod Co-Leaders, and the employee align on growth. Never tied to compensation.

**Action Item** — A discrete, owned commitment that emerges from a retrospective. Reviewed at the next retrospective before any new data is discussed.

**Daily Practice** — The small repeated habit that builds the muscle behind a Pivotal Value.

**Defining Moment** — What a manager does when the value is hardest to live by. The test, not the routine.

**Department** — The career home. Where craft mastery, professional identity, and the promotion path live.

**FeedForward** — Forward-looking development input — "what could they do more of next month?" — used in place of backward-looking critique.

**Functional Leader** — The Department head. Accountable for skill standards, performance reviews, and promotion recommendations.

**Matrix Organization** — A structure in which every employee answers to two managers — a Functional Leader and Pod Co-Leaders — by design, not accident.

**Peripheral Value** — A pod-specific norm. How each team expresses the core identity. Loose, adaptable, owned by Pod Co-Leaders.

**Pivotal Value** — A non-negotiable, company-wide commitment. Tight. Identical in Carlsbad, New York, and Honolulu.

**Pod** — The cultural home. A persistent group of leaders co-leading multiple projects, where daily belonging is felt.

**Pod Co-Leader** — One of two leaders (typically SPM + FOM) who jointly own a pod's daily execution and team health.

**Pod Scorecard** — A monthly view of pod-level schedule, budget, safety, and client-satisfaction data. Displayed for cohesion, not competition.

**Project Team** — The execution home. A temporary group assembled for a single build, dissolved when the build is done.

**RAPID** — A decision-rights framework: Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide. Exactly one Decide per recurring decision.

**SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact)** — A feedback structure: name the situation, describe the behavior, state the impact. Used in recognition and coaching alike.

**SMART Goal** — A specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound objective. Set in the annual review, revisited monthly.

**Team Health Pulse** — A monthly anonymous survey aggregated at the pod level. Frequency-scaled, ~15–17 questions, ~18 minutes. Measures collective experience over time.

**Two-Manager Model** — DCC's matrix in practice: Functional Leader owns career and standards; Pod Co-Leaders own daily work and team health.
